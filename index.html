Section 4: General Information - Part 1: Philippine Constitution

Passing Score: 8/10

Direction: Choose the correct answer from the choices given.

1. What article of the 1987 Philippine Constitution defines the national territory of the Philippines?

  • A) Article III
  • B) Article II
  • C) Article I
  • D) Article X
  • E) Article IV

2. Who has the power to declare war under the 1987 Philippine Constitution?

  • A) The President
  • B) The Armed Forces
  • C) The Supreme Court
  • D) The Congress
  • E) The Vice President

3. Which right is protected under the Bill of Rights in the 1987 Philippine Constitution?

  • A) Right to bear arms without restriction
  • B) Right to private education only
  • C) Right to invade privacy for national security
  • D) Freedom of speech
  • E) Right to indefinite detention

4. Who appoints the justices of the Supreme Court in the Philippines?

  • A) The Senate
  • B) The House of Representatives
  • C) The Judicial and Bar Council
  • D) The President
  • E) The Chief Justice

5. According to the Constitution, who is considered a natural-born Filipino citizen?

  • A) Someone born in any Asian country
  • B) A person born abroad to Filipino parents
  • C) Anyone who lives in the Philippines for 5 years
  • D) A foreigner married to a Filipino
  • E) Someone who speaks Filipino fluently

6. What constitutional body is responsible for conducting elections in the Philippines?

  • A) Commission on Audit
  • B) Civil Service Commission
  • C) Department of the Interior and Local Government
  • D) Commission on Elections
  • E) Department of Justice

7. Under the 1987 Constitution, what is the primary purpose of the Ombudsman?

  • A) Manage national defense
  • B) Regulate foreign trade
  • C) Investigate and prosecute corrupt public officials
  • D) Approve international treaties
  • E) Supervise all elections

8. Which article focuses on the rights of laborers and social justice?

  • A) Article VI
  • B) Article XI
  • C) Article XIII
  • D) Article XV
  • E) Article IX

9. How long is the term of office for the President of the Philippines under the 1987 Constitution?

  • A) 4 years, renewable
  • B) 6 years, with one re-election
  • C) 6 years, without re-election
  • D) 5 years, unlimited terms
  • E) 3 years, renewable indefinitely

10. Which of the following is a power of judicial review given to the Supreme Court?

  • A) Declaring laws unconstitutional
  • B) Vetoing executive orders
  • C) Appointing senators
  • D) Approving the national budget
  • E) Conducting impeachment trials
